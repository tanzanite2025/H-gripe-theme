Brass vs. Alloy Nipples: How to Choose
When configuring a wheelset, you can choose between brass or alloy nipples. Below we compare their differences in five aspects to help you make the right decision:

1. Weight
- Brass nipples: about 1 gram each
- Alloy nipples: about 0.33 gram each
- While the difference per nipple may seem small, a wheelset can have up to 72 nipples, and since they are positioned near the outer diameter of the wheel, the effect on rotational weight is significant.

2. Color
- Brass: cannot be anodized, limited color options (usually black or silver), black brass tends to fade more easily
- Alloy: can be anodized, available in a wide range of colors, more vibrant appearance

3. Strength
- Standard 12mm brass nipples: traditional head design, generally stronger overall
- Alloy nipples with extended heads (e.g., double square or hex head): much higher shear strength due to oversized head and no slot; 16mm versions can achieve full thread engagement, whereas traditional nipples typically only achieve about two-thirds engagement

4. Building Performance
- Alloy nipples have less friction when threading onto steel spokes, reducing spoke wind-up during assembly
- Alloy is softer than brass, so nipples can round off more easily; using a 4-sided external wrench or internal tools (like DT Squorx) is recommended
- High-end alloy nipples (e.g., Sapim Polyax) are made from heat-treated 7075 alloy, making them harder and more durable
- Locking features (e.g., Sapim Secure Lock, DT Pro Lock) help prevent loosening and loss of tension
- Tapered heads reduce stress at the nipple bed and align better with the spokes

5. Corrosion Resistance
- Brass: generally more resistant to corrosion
- Alloy: more prone to galvanic corrosion when paired with carbon rims; only high-quality alloy nipples should be used
- High-end alloy nipples (e.g., Sapim) receive special anti-corrosion treatment, and salt spray tests show even better results than nickel-plated brass nipples

✅ Summary Recommendations
- For lightweight and color options → Choose alloy nipples
- For durability and corrosion resistance → Choose brass nipples
- For carbon rims → Use high-quality alloy or brass nipples; avoid low-end alloy nipples due to corrosion risk
