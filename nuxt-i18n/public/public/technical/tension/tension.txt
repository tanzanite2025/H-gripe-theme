Wheel Spoke Tension and Rider Weight/Usage (Workshop Standard)  【蓝色文本标题】
1. Effect of Rider Weight   【黄色文本】
Static load: For example, when a 75 kg rider sits on the bike, the tension in the upper spokes increases by about 15%, while the tension in the lower spokes decreases.
Dynamic riding: During pedaling, the drive-side spokes gain tension while the braking-side spokes lose tension. The distribution changes continuously with rider movement.
Heavier riders: Require higher overall spoke tension and thicker spokes to prevent the wheel from deforming or losing stiffness under load.

Our Workshop Process (Practical Standard)  【蓝色文本标题】
When building wheels, we strictly follow this workflow:
Weight classification → Determine the baseline tension range.
Usage adjustment → Modify tension according to riding purpose【前面已经有了蓝色与黄色文本，如果再加颜色会显得很难看，是否在左右加个比如这种大括号】
Road racing → higher tension for stiffness.
Mountain biking → slightly lower tension for durability.
Touring → balanced tension for stability.
 Material limits →【前面已经有了蓝色与黄色文本，如果再加颜色会显得很难看，是否在左右加个比如这种大括号】
 Carbon rims → allow higher tension (up to 135kgf).
Alloy rims → capped at 120–125kgf.
Spoke selection → Match spoke gauge to rider weight and usage.
Final build → Ensure proper drive/non-drive tension ratio (about 10:6–10:7,Some hub geometries may only be 10:4) and use rim offset to balance.

 Conclusion  【蓝色文本标题】
This is our actual workshop standard, not just theory:
